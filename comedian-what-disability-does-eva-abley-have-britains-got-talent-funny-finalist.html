Comedian: What Disability Does Eva Abley Have? Britains Got Talent Funny Finalist

Eva Abley is a British comedian who made it to the finals of Britain’s Got Talent Series 15. Please stick with us to know about her disability which she revealed in her audition.

Abley had her first taste of stand-up comedy when she entered a talent contest while on a camping trip to Newquay.

Her comic timing was hailed as “amazing” by Amanda, who added, “you’re so vibrant and charming, it’s really pleasant to watch you.”

BGT: What Disability Does Eva Abley Have?

Eva Abley, a 14-year-old cerebral palsy sufferer, was another disadvantaged artist to grace the BGT stage.

She performed a “vivacious, charming, and simply funny” comedy performance in which she defied several disability stereotypes and reminded audiences that we are all diverse and unique.

Abley made comments about the perks of her disability in the Semi-Finals, including how she got free Botox, which she assumed her parents were envious of.

She then went on to say something about how she didn’t think she should be trusted to learn to drive or steer anything, before going on to say something about Simon’s electric bike crash.

She then went on to make additional statements regarding Prime Minister Boris Johnson to round out her act. She was one of the top three acts of the year.

About Eva Abley Cerebral Palsy And Health Condition

Eva Abley made comments regarding living with Cerebral Palsy at her audition, citing the positives of being home-schooled and her concerns about being ‘anything you want to be.’

A cerebral palsy is a group of disorders that affect a person’s ability to move, balance, and maintain their posture. It is the most common motor disability in children.

It is a term that refers to something having to do with the brain. It is caused by improper brain development or damage to the growing brain, impairs a person’s ability to control his or her muscles.
