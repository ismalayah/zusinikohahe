Michaela Conlin Early life and education

Born June 9, 1978, in Allentown, PA, Michaela Conlin has a diverse heritage: a Chinese mother, an Irish father.

There, she showcased her talent by taking on roles in plays like “Bye Bye Birdie” and “The Crucible.”

After high school in 1996, Michaela pursued her acting passion in bustling New York City.

She successfully gained admission to the prestigious New York University Tisch School of the Arts.

During her time at NYU, she diligently pursued her Bachelor of Fine Arts in Theater.

Throughout her academic journey, Michaela Conlin’s talent continued to shine as she graced the stages of various renowned theaters and institutions.

She showcased her acting skills in productions at the Atlantic Theater Company, Playwrights Horizons, and Stonestreet Studios.
